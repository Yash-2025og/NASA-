NASA Rockets & Spacecraft

The powerful launch vehicles and spacecraft that have made space exploration possible, from the early days of space travel to today's cutting-edge technology.

Saturn V

Height: 111 m (363 ft)

Thrust: 7.6 million pounds

Missions: Apollo program

The Saturn V remains the tallest, heaviest, and most powerful rocket ever brought to operational status, used to launch astronauts to the Moon during the Apollo program.

Space Launch System (SLS)

Height: 98 m (322 ft)

Thrust: 8.8 million pounds

Missions: Artemis program

NASA's newest heavy-lift rocket designed to send humans to the Moon and beyond as part of the Artemis program.

Space Shuttle

Height: 56 m (184 ft)

Thrust: 6.6 million pounds

Missions: 135 flights (1981-2011)

The world's first reusable spacecraft, the Space Shuttle carried crews and cargo to orbit for three decades, including deploying the Hubble Telescope and building the ISS.

X-15

Speed: 7,274 km/h (4,520 mph)

Altitude: 107.8 km (354,200 ft)

Missions: 199 flights (1959-1968)

The rocket-powered X-15 set speed and altitude records in the 1960s, becoming the first winged aircraft to reach the edge of space.

Space Exploration Timeline

Key milestones in NASA's journey of discovery and innovation throughout the decades.

1958

NASA Founded

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration was established on July 29, 1958.

1969

Apollo 11 Moon Landing

"That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." Neil Armstrong becomes the first human to walk on the Moon.

1990

Hubble Space Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope is deployed, providing unprecedented views of distant galaxies.

2004

Mars Exploration Rovers

Spirit and Opportunity rovers land on Mars, beginning a new era of Mars exploration.

2021

Mars Helicopter Flight

Ingenuity makes the first powered flight on another planet, taking off from the Martian surface.

About NASA

NASA, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is America's civil space program and the global leader in space exploration. The agency has a diverse workforce of just under 18,000 civil servants, and works with many more U.S. contractors, academia, and international and commercial partners to explore, discover, and expand knowledge for the benefit of humanity.
